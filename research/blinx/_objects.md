# _objects.md — Reconstruction-Grade Object Teardowns

Standard of this file: enough adjectives and relational descriptors per object
that a builder who has NEVER seen the source image could recreate it and land
inside the style. Proportions are relational ("shoulder twice the broken
side"), colors are paint-mixing names, every surface is given as an ordered
list of layers, every object ends with its TELL. Method: _method.md. Style
context: _style.md.

---

## 1. THE ROCK (Everwinter) — w7e-rocksnowcap

**Silhouette:** a slumped loaf; a drowsing animal lying with its back to you.
One long lazy shoulder slopes away at roughly a rooftop's pitch; the other
side is a short, stepped, broken cliff. Nothing level, nothing centered —
the mass clearly sits heavier on one end. The top line is a slow wave, not a
peak.

**Build:** 6–9 big flat planes, each the size of a serving platter or bigger —
no small chipping. Planes meet at angles you could measure with a school
protractor. The whole mass reads carved from one block, not stacked.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) flat value per facet — that is all.
(2) snow, applied by RULE: any facet facing up enough gets a white cap.
(3) nothing else. The dark facets are naked — no noise, no speckle, no
gradient.

**Value & hue:** lit planes = warm cocoa-taupe, like milk chocolate left in
the sun. Shade planes = bitter dark chocolate, nearly purple-brown. Snow =
cream-white with the faintest blue breath in its shadowed hem. Three values,
one family plus white.

**Edges:** facet seams are hard and honest. The snow cap's lower edge is a
hand-wobbled hem, like a blanket thrown over furniture — it dips and lifts
per facet, never ruler-straight, never the same wave twice.

**Placement:** buried. The lowest facets vanish under the snow drift; there
is no contact line, no skirt, no shadow decal shouting "I sit here." The
drift swallows it whole.

**Mood:** patient, animal, asleep. A boulder that has been sitting so long
the weather upholstered it.

**Tell:** you can count the planes, each plane owns exactly one value, and
the snow is a per-plane rule — not an asset sprinkled on top.

## 2. THE ICE PILLAR (Everwinter) — w7e-icepillar

**Silhouette:** a frozen geyser; a candle of glacier. A tall column, swollen
at the base, tapering gently to a rounded waist, leaning a few degrees off
vertical like it's listening to something.

**Build:** a lathe — but a lathe whose sides you can count (8–12), left
unsmoothed so the column flashes long flat strips of value as it turns. One
piece, no segments stacked.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) one big soft vertical gradient: breath-pale
at the top, deepening to a drowned blue at the base — the ground's darkness
rising up into the ice. (2) a blush: the left flank carries a pink-magenta
wash, the color of the horizon sky, as if dawn got trapped inside. (3) no
texture. Zero. The gradients do everything.

**Value & hue:** pale ice-blue → steel blue → bruise blue down the length;
the blush is cold pink, like a cheek in winter.

**Edges:** silhouette edges are smooth but not machined — the countable
chords of the lathe keep it honest. No bevel highlight; the gradient is the
shading.

**Placement:** stands in snow like a tooth in gum — base swallowed, a drift
collar lapping at it.

**Mood:** monolithic, calm, faintly sacred — a standing stone made of weather.

**Tell:** the pink flank. Objects in Blinx carry their sky; if the pillar
is only blue, it's not finished.

## 3. THE ICICLE STRIP (Everwinter) — w7e-icicles

**Silhouette:** a fringe of teeth drawn by a child — some long and greedy,
some stubby, none equal, all leaning slightly the same way like wind combed
them once and they froze mid-comb.

**Build:** a jagged alpha strip or a row of small tapered shards hung along
the UNDERSIDE of a warm timber edge. Thickest clusters at corners and joints
— wherever water would gather.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) pale body — milk-glass white-blue.
(2) dabs of deeper blue in the shadowed crotch between teeth. Two values.

**Value & hue:** body = skim milk with a blue thought; shadow dabs = cold
steel blue. Against the warm honey-brown timber they hang from, the contrast
is the whole effect.

**Edges:** each tooth tapers to a blunt point — never needle-sharp; these are
candy icicles, not hazards. The strip's overall lower line is a confident
zigzag.

**Placement:** under eaves, gate edges, any horizontal warm surface that
overhangs cold air. Always attached, always underside.

**Mood:** cheerful cold — decoration the weather made.

**Tell:** two values only, and the thickest teeth sit exactly where the
structure's corners are.

## 4. THE CANDY WALL + ICING EMBLEM (Everwinter) — w7e-candywall

**Silhouette:** a vast flat crimson wall — a cookie stood on end — flanked by
two fat striped pillars. Centered on the wall: a symmetric piped-frosting
curlicue, confectionery heraldry, like a royal cipher squeezed from a piping
bag.

**Build:** the wall is one flat plane — all richness is mounted ON it, none
IN it. The emblem is made of thick rounded TUBES, torus-section, looping in
mirror symmetry. The pillars are cylinders with only 3–4 wide horizontal
bands per height — chunky stripes, not barber-pole fine.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) crimson flat field — deep as velvet
curtain. (2) emblem tubes: deep teal body, one soft bevel — pale cyan kiss
along the top of each tube, darker teal shadow beneath. (3) pillars:
alternating red and white bands, edges soft, profile slightly swollen.
(4) air: small white diamond sparkles drifting everywhere in front.

**Value & hue:** crimson + teal + white — a triadic chord at full saturation;
the air between is pink-lavender. No mud anywhere.

**Edges:** the emblem glows faintly — a cyan-ish bloom softening its outline.
Band edges on the pillars are soft, like fondant, not taped.

**Placement:** the emblem sits at the wall's visual center like a crest;
pillars bracket the composition; sparkles fill the volume between wall and
camera.

**Mood:** festive, edible, faintly ceremonial — a cathedral of sugar.

**Tell:** the wall itself is EMPTY. Confidence = one flat field + one
ornament + stripes + sparkle. Fine stripes or wall texture would kill it.

## 5. THE STREET LAMP (Time Square) — w1ts-lamp

**Silhouette:** a stem that grew toward the light and forgot to stop. A thin
pole, tapered, bent in one long S — not kinked, not broken, GROWN. The head
is tiny: a little lantern no bigger than a teacup perched at the tip.

**Build:** pole = a few bent straight segments (you can count the chords of
the curve), verdigris green like old copper. Head = a small caged glow.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) green pole, one value per segment, slightly
paler where it catches the lamp's own light. (2) the lantern: warm amber
core. (3) the halo: a bloom 4–5× the fixture's size that swallows the top of
the stem whole.

**Value & hue:** pole = murky green-grey; light = amber-hot center fading
through honey to nothing. Against the night street, the lamp is the brightest
thing in frame.

**Edges:** pole edges crisp; halo edges feather to zero.

**Placement:** at path edges and corners — where a walker would want it.
Always near a pool of its own light.

**Mood:** lonely but dutiful. A small public servant.

**Tell:** the halo is bigger than the lantern and eats the stem; and the pole
is never, ever straight.

## 6. THE LAMP LIGHT POOL — lamppool

**Silhouette:** a soft-edged ellipse of warmth laid on the stone like a rug.

**Build:** a flat painted gradient on the ground plane — no geometry. Sized
generously: the pool is several times wider than the lamp's pole is tall.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) amber heart. (2) honey mid. (3) a rim that
dissolves into the ground's own mottle. The ground texture shows THROUGH the
pool — it is a tint, not a carpet.

**Value & hue:** warm gold over cool stone; the meeting of the two is where
the world's temperature contrast lives.

**Edges:** the outer edge is crisp enough to read as a boundary — you know
when you're IN the light — but feathered, never hard.

**Placement:** directly under its source, sometimes clipped by a curb or
wall base, which makes it feel physical.

**Mood:** safety. A small room made of light.

**Tell:** pools are the path. Chain them and the player follows without a
single arrow.

## 7. THE FLOWER PLANTER (Time Square) — w1ts-flowers

**Silhouette:** a low stone trough, waist-high, with two loose rows of tulips
standing in it like a choir that hasn't rehearsed.

**Build:** the planter box is worn stone: a thick pale rim cap, a stained
body, dark soil inside. Each tulip = a thin stem + one cup. About ten cups.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) stone body with age stains — vertical
streaks like old rain. (2) pale rim, the lightest stone in the arrangement.
(3) soil: near-black, lumpy. (4) cups: red-orange with yellow tips, as if
each flower were dipped in flame. (5) per-flower lean jitter — no two stems
agree.

**Value & hue:** grey stone + near-black soil + hot red-orange + yellow.
The flowers are the only warm accent; everything else is a foil.

**Edges:** rim edge hard; flower cups soft and simple — no petal detail, a
cup shape and color do the work.

**Placement:** against house walls, at doorsteps, flanking paths — wherever a
resident would tend them. A garden implies a gardener; that implication is
the point.

**Mood:** domestic tenderness in a crooked midnight world.

**Tell:** two rows, ten cups, lean jitter, flame-dipped tips. And the stain
streaks on the box — nobody's planter is clean.

## 8. THE STATUE (Time Square) — w1ts-statue

**Silhouette:** a reclining figure, heavy and calm, like someone resting
after a long century. The mass is low, the outline lumpy and eroded — no
sharp anatomy survives.

**Build:** one carved mass on a base of fractured slabs. The fracture lines
are real geometry — the base is broken into 2–3 pieces that have settled
apart slightly.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) stone mid-value, grey with a warm thought.
(2) dark speckle blotches scattered like age-spots. (3) green moss WASH laid
over every up-facing surface — shoulders, brow, knees — as if the sky
seasoned it. (4) painted recess shade: dark dabs under the chin, in the
folds, under the arms — the crevices are PAINTED, not rendered.

**Value & hue:** warm grey + moss green + near-black recesses. Three notes.

**Edges:** silhouette eroded-soft; recess edges painted hard for contrast.

**Placement:** the fractured slabs double as the anti-float skirt — the
statue's broken base keys it into the plaza.

**Mood:** venerable, forgotten, patient. Time's own mascot in a game about
time.

**Tell:** moss only where the sky touches; speckle everywhere; crevices
painted darker than any light would naturally make them.

## 9. THE WINDOW CARDS (Time Square) — w1ts-windows

**Silhouette:** crooked yellow parallelograms stuck on the walls like notes
on a fridge — none level, none the same tilt, gathered in sociable clusters
of 2–5.

**Build:** flat glowing cards applied to the wall plane. The mullions — the
cross bars — are PAINTED ON the card as dark strokes, and they're as wonky
as the cards: slightly bent, slightly off-center, hand-done.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) warm yellow field, butter-bright.
(2) dark mullion strokes. (3) sometimes a faint warm bloom bleeding past the
card's edge onto the wall.

**Value & hue:** hot butter yellow against the olive-dark walls — the windows
are the town's hearth-light made visible.

**Edges:** card edges hard; bloom soft. The parallelogram skew is the
signature — every window leans with its house's gesture.

**Placement:** clustered, following no floor logic — some high, some low, as
if rooms were added whenever someone felt like it.

**Mood:** inhabited warmth; someone is home behind every crooked card.

**Tell:** mullions painted ON the glow, clusters not grids, every card
sharing its building's lean.

## 10. THE ARCH HOUSE (Time Square) — w1ts-arch

**Silhouette:** a cottage settling down like a tired animal sitting. The
whole mass leans left as ONE gesture — roof, walls, and door all agree on
the lean. The roofline sags in the middle like a spine.

**Build:** hand-stacked stone — uneven blocks, some proud, some sunken. The
door is a red CURTAIN, off-center, bulging outward slightly as if the house
is exhaling. Windows are the glowing crooked cards (entry 9). At the base:
clumps of flowers, patchy stone-and-dirt ground.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) stone in an olive-grey family, each block
its own value. (2) grime wash at the base. (3) the curtain: deep red fabric,
one soft fold-shadow. (4) window cards glowing over everything.

**Value & hue:** olive-grey mass + red curtain heart + yellow window sparks.
The curtain is the warm center; the windows are the jewelry.

**Edges:** the silhouette's top is a sagging wave; corners are soft, worn,
rounded like soap.

**Placement:** sits ON its ground, base partially sunk into the dirt patch —
the house has weight and has pressed itself into the earth over centuries.

**Mood:** weary domesticity. A house that has earned its lean.

**Tell:** the whole building shares ONE lean — and the door is fabric, not
wood. Softness where you expect hardness is the signature.

## 11. THE SHOPFRONT (Time Square) — w1ts-shop

**Silhouette:** a warped, rippling awning like a flag in wind that froze
mid-flap; a bent striped pole holding it up, bowed like a fishing rod.

**Build:** awning = a rippled sheet with bold stripes, attached above the
shop opening. Pole = bent segments. On the brick beside: neon green glyph
tags, flat and floating like stickers. A sign card (orange, warped) carries
painted text.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) awning stripes, two hues, high contrast.
(2) pole stripes, thinner, candy-like. (3) the neon glyphs: pure flat
green-cyan, glowing, slightly bloomed. (4) a hot pink glow from somewhere
above — an unseen sign bleeding color into the scene.

**Value & hue:** the muted brick is a stage; awning + neon + pink bleed are
the performers. Saturation lives on the flat graphics, not the building.

**Edges:** awning edge is a confident wave; glyph edges glow-soft.

**Placement:** at street level, on the walking route — commerce where feet
are.

**Mood:** street-corner liveliness in a medieval night — the future's
graffiti on the past's wall.

**Tell:** ripples and bows everywhere, but the graphics are FLAT. The world
warps; the signage doesn't.

## 12. THE PENNANT SHAPES (Time Square) — w1ts-pennant

**Silhouette:** enormous flat shapes — yellow-orange, hard-edged — hung
against the dark tower like paper cutouts pinned to a curtain. Matisse
shapes: bold, simple, slightly irregular, unmistakably 2D.

**Build:** flat cards. No thickness, no shading, no texture. One hue each.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) flat yellow-orange. That is the entire
recipe.

**Value & hue:** hot marigold against the tower's near-black — maximum
figure-ground punch with zero rendering.

**Edges:** hard and clean, with tiny hand-cut irregularities — the edge of
scissors, not the edge of a vector.

**Placement:** high on the tower, arranged like bunting or flags — festive
height.

**Mood:** celebration in the dark; a party the town threw and nobody took
down.

**Tell:** they do not conform to the 3D — they FLOAT in it, shamelessly
flat. That shamelessness is the voice of the Time Factory's graphic
language.

## 13. THE TIME CRYSTAL (everywhere) — w1ts-crystal

**Silhouette:** a crescent — a moon-shard, a broken piece of a circle —
glowing, floating. An icon, not a mineral. You would recognize it as a
SYMBOL before you'd call it a rock.

**Build:** a flat-ish extruded crescent shape, spinning slowly, bobbing.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) pure saturated cyan, self-lit. (2) a
bloom halo bleeding past its edges. Nothing else — no facets, no veins, no
refraction pretense.

**Value & hue:** the single most saturated cyan in the scene, staged against
the murkiest available ground (brown-green dark) so it detonates.

**Edges:** shape edges crisp, halo feathered.

**Placement:** hovering at reachable height along paths and near secrets —
they are breadcrumbs and rewards.

**Mood:** precious, alien, game-like — a piece of frozen time you can
pocket.

**Tell:** it is a flat graphic that spins. The pickup language and the
pennant language are the same language.

## 14. THE IVY MASS (Time Square) — w1ts-ivy

**Silhouette:** a dark, ragged, creeping blot — lichen with ambition. It
climbs the wall in an irregular tide-mark shape, densest at the base and
around window frames, thinning into stray fingers higher up.

**Build:** layered flat dabs and blotch clusters applied to the wall — many
small marks aggregated into one big organic mass. It does not respect the
brick courses beneath; it eats them.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) deep shadow-green underlayer, the mass's
body. (2) mid-green dabs over it. (3) sparse pale tips where leaves catch
light. (4) beneath it all, the wall still faintly visible at the mass's
thin edges.

**Value & hue:** swamp-green family — near-black body, moss mid, bone-pale
tips. Darker than anything man-made in the same frame.

**Edges:** ragged everywhere; the mass boundary is a broken tide-line, never
a clean shape.

**Placement:** wall bases, window surrounds, anywhere damp and old. It
always grows FROM somewhere — ground, sill, seam.

**Mood:** beautiful decay; the world quietly reclaiming itself.

**Tell:** it ignores the man-made grid beneath it — organic blotch over
brick courses. That disobedience is how age is painted in Blinx.

## 15. THE BRICK WALL (Déjà Vu Canals) — w2-brick

**Silhouette:** a big warm wall plane, corners chamfered (never a hard 90°),
the surface alive like a hand-laid quilt.

**Build:** individual bricks — each one placed with jittered size, slight
rotation, small gaps. Coursing is irregular: some bricks run long, some
short, a few nearly vertical, as if several masons with different habits
worked the same wall. Mortar lines are soft and wavering, never a crisp
grid.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) per-brick value: every brick carries its
own micro pillow-shade — pale top edge, dark bottom edge — thousands of
tiny bevels baked in. (2) per-brick hue drift inside ONE narrow family:
sandy olive — some bricks paler, some pinker, some greyer. (3) big shadow
PATCHES painted ACROSS the courses, soft-edged, ignoring brick boundaries
entirely — the shade of things you can't see, cast onto the wall.

**Value & hue:** sand, khaki, faint rose, pale olive — a warm narrow chord;
the shadow patches drop whole regions one value step darker.

**Edges:** brick bevels micro-soft; shadow patch edges feathered; wall
corners chamfered hard.

**Placement:** everywhere — but the wall never reads as texture-tile because
of the macro shadow patches.

**Mood:** sunlit age; a wall that has watched a canal for centuries.

**Tell:** micro-detail per brick + macro shade over everything. The two
scales never acknowledge each other, and that is exactly why it works.

## 16. THE CANAL COPING (Déjà Vu Canals) — w2-coping

**Silhouette:** a pale chunky outline tracing every water edge — the canal
drawn with a light pen.

**Build:** overhanging cap slabs laid along the rim of every wall that meets
water. Each slab is a block with slight per-slab value jitter; the overhang
creates a dark seam underneath.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) pale stone — the lightest value in the
waterside palette. (2) per-slab jitter so no two caps match exactly.
(3) the under-seam: a dark line where cap overhangs wall — instant
thickness.

**Value & hue:** bone-pale caps over mid-value walls over dark water: a
three-step value ladder at every edge.

**Edges:** cap edges crisp; the under-seam is a hard dark line — the hardest
edge in the waterside vocabulary.

**Placement:** on EVERY water boundary. Water in Blinx is always framed.

**Mood:** crafted, maintained, loved — the city finishes its edges.

**Tell:** the palest thing at the water's edge is always the cap, and the
darkest line in the wall is always right under it.

## 17. THE FOUNTAIN (Déjà Vu Canals) — w2-fountain

**Silhouette:** an octagonal basin on an octagonal plinth, with a shallow
pedestal bowl rising from the middle — a wedding cake drawn by a
woodcarver. Nothing round: every "circle" has eight honest sides.

**Build:** plinth slab → basin ring → pedestal → bowl. Four stacked parts,
all faceted octagons. Pickups (stars, gems) sit IN the bowl — dressing with
a function.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) plinth and coping rim: the palest stone
in the scene (the coping rule — the cap is always the light). (2) basin
body: much darker, so the rim reads as a lit outline ring. (3) water: a
flat opaque teal disc — maybe a slight radial gradient, no waves, no
transparency. Water is a COLOR, not a shader. (4) bowl interior shadowed.

**Value & hue:** pale rim / dark body / teal water — three clean notes.

**Edges:** facet seams hard; the water's edge where it meets the basin is a
clean ellipse boundary.

**Placement:** plaza center, raised on its plinth — nothing in Blinx sits on
bare ground; everything gets a step or a plinth.

**Mood:** civic pride; the town's jewel.

**Tell:** eight sides, palest rim, flat teal water, pickups in the bowl.

## 18. THE TEMPLE RELIEF (Temple of Lost Time) — w5t-reliefzoom

**Silhouette:** a flat wall carrying an interlocking frieze of serpentine
glyphs — a maze drawn by a river god. The design meanders asymmetrically;
no tile repeats.

**Build:** the relief is FAKE — flat wall, painted depth. Each glyph is one
shape with a two-value bevel trick.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) wall base: deep green-olive. (2) glyph
body: one mid value, yellow-green. (3) glyph top edges: a pale kiss of
highlight. (4) glyph under-edges: a dark olive shadow-dab. Top light +
bottom dark = "carved" — from two extra values. (5) the whole wall soaked
in ONE green grade so relief and flat share a single light.

**Value & hue:** olive dark / moss mid / pale yellow-green highlight — one
family, three steps.

**Edges:** glyph outlines hard; bevel transitions painted soft within the
glyph.

**Placement:** frieze bands at eye level and above — where a walker would
read them.

**Mood:** ancient meaning; a language you almost understand.

**Tell:** up close it is completely flat and unashamed. The carve exists
only as a top-light/bottom-dark agreement, repeated with total consistency.

## 19. THE FLOOR TILES (Temple of Lost Time) — w5t-tiles

**Silhouette:** a grid — but a grid that age has been voting against.

**Build:** big square tiles, each one pillow-shaded: pale center, darkened
on all four edges, near-black grout between. The pillow shading makes every
tile read slightly domed, like the lid of a butter dish.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) per-tile base value with drift — some
tiles warmer, some cooler. (2) the pillow shade: center pale, edges dark.
(3) grout: near-black lines, slightly wavering. (4) moss: whole tiles
recolored green, OFF-GRID — a tile either belongs to the moss or it
doesn't; there are no gradient negotiations across tile boundaries.
(5) worn pale centers on the walking line — feet have polished a path.

**Value & hue:** stone mid + moss green + black grout + bone wear-path.

**Edges:** grout lines hard; pillow shading soft; moss boundaries follow
tile edges exactly.

**Placement:** the walking surface — the wear path IS the level design
drawn in value.

**Mood:** walked-on for a thousand years.

**Tell:** moss recolors WHOLE tiles, never bleeds between them — organic
age that still respects the grid it conquers, one tile at a time.

## 20. THE MOON + SKY LAYERS (Time Square) — w1ts-moon

**Silhouette:** a gigantic pale disc, low in the sky, cut into by the
roofline — crenellations and tower tops biting pieces out of it. The
occlusion is the point: a moon behind things is a moon with a world in
front of it.

**Build:** the moon is a flat disc on a far layer — deliberately SOFT,
blurrier and lower-detail than everything in front of it. One darker
mottled continent blot is painted on it — ONE irregular shape, not crater
noise. Foreground: chunky merlon blocks whose arrow slots are PAINTED dark
shapes, not holes. Sky: a vertical gradient.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) sky: deep violet at the top warming to
a murkier glow at the horizon. (2) the moon disc: pale lavender-pink,
flat, soft-edged. (3) one continent blot, mauve-grey, irregular. (4)
clouds: 2–3 flat soft shapes, same soft treatment. (5) foreground
silhouette: the roofline, near-black, sharp.

**Value & hue:** violet deep → lavender pale → mauve blot → near-black
foreground: a four-layer value descent into distance.

**Edges:** moon edge feather-soft; foreground crenellation edges hard. The
SHARP thing cuts the SOFT thing — that cut is the depth.

**Placement:** the moon hangs LOW so architecture always overlaps it; it is
staging, not astronomy.

**Mood:** hushed, enormous, faintly wrong — a storybook watched by
something.

**Tell:** sharpness is spent ONLY up close; the far giant is blurred on
purpose, and it is always partially hidden behind something small.

## 21. THE FLAME (Mine / Forge) — w6m-fire

**Silhouette:** a lumpy bunch of grapes made of light — 5–10 blobs stacked,
biggest at the base, the whole cluster wobbling and stretching.

**Build:** not one sprite: a CLUSTER of additive soft quads, each a radial
gradient blob, stacked and animated (stretch, wobble, per-frame variation).
The cluster is bigger than the player — Blinx fire is chunky and generous.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) core: near-white hot heart, bottom
center. (2) mid: yellow. (3) edges: orange. Each blob carries the same
white→yellow→orange ramp; stacked, they make a flame with a hot center of
gravity.

**Value & hue:** near-white → butter → ember orange, additive — and it is
ALWAYS staged against the darkest available backdrop (deep green-black
murk), because fire is a value event and needs a void to burn in.

**Edges:** every blob feather-soft; the cluster's outer silhouette is lumpy
and alive.

**Placement:** in braziers, vents, pits — always with a dark surround and
usually a warm pool on nearby ground.

**Mood:** dangerous warmth; the dark world's hearth.

**Tell:** count the blobs. A single shaped sprite is the wrong answer —
Blinx fire is a bunch of soft lights arguing.

## 22. THE CYAN GLOW POOL (Mine) — w6m-cyanpool

**Silhouette:** a crisp-edged ellipse of cold light lying on the ground —
a dropped piece of sky.

**Build:** a flat painted ellipse under every glow source: flat fill,
slight inner gradient, and a bright RIM RING around its edge — the rim is
the signature.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) cyan fill, slightly stronger at center.
(2) the bright rim ring, the lightest cyan, crisp. (3) ground mottle
showing faintly through.

**Value & hue:** electric cyan on near-black ground — the mine's whole
navigation system written in one hue.

**Edges:** outer rim crisp (unlike the warm lamp pools, which feather) —
cold light gets a hard boundary in this vocabulary.

**Placement:** under crystals, lamps, machinery glows — always with its
source, always on walkable ground.

**Mood:** clinical wonder; technology glowing in the dark.

**Tell:** the rim ring. A glow pool without a bright rim is unfinished.

## 23. THE FORGE BEAMS + RIVETS (Forge) — w8f-beams

**Silhouette:** big flat orange plates crossing at confident angles —
industrial carpentry, chunky and overlapped.

**Build:** each beam is a flat plate with a single value per face. Where
beams join: rivets — and each rivet is a three-mark painting.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) plate face: hot orange, one value.
(2) plate edges: darker orange-brown seam shadows where plates overlap.
(3) each rivet: a red disc + a dark crescent on the lower side + a hot dab
on the upper-left. Three marks, one light direction, repeated thousands of
times without deviation.

**Value & hue:** ember orange family + rust red + near-black seams. The
upper-left hot dabs are the brightest repeated marks in the world.

**Edges:** plate edges hard; rivet shading painted soft within the disc.

**Placement:** beams brace openings and span gaps; rivets march along every
join like stitching.

**Mood:** honest industry — a machine age you could maintain with a wrench.

**Tell:** the rivet's hot dab is ALWAYS upper-left. One sun, one world, no
exceptions — consistency of light direction is what makes painted detail
read as real.

## 24. THE GROUND LAYER STACK (Hourglass Caves) — w4c-ground

**Silhouette:** the floor itself — a painted terrain of tile, drift, and
smear.

**Build:** FOUR painted layers, each with a job, composited on the ground
plane.

**Surface & paint, in order:** (1) base: a crackle tile — small irregular
fractured plates, like dried mud or old glaze, mid-value warm grey.
(2) drift: pale blobs pooling in low spots and edges — dust or sand that
moved in and stayed. (3) swept smears: directional streaks, all leaning
one way — the memory of wind or water passing through. (4) contact shadow
blobs: soft dark dabs under every rock, pot, and prop — each object gets
one.

**Value & hue:** warm grey base / bone-pale drifts / darker directional
smears / near-black contact dabs — four values, four jobs.

**Edges:** crackle edges hard; drift edges feathered; smears soft and
directional; contact dabs soft-centered with crisp-ish cores.

**Placement:** smears agree on ONE direction per area; drifts collect where
physics would put them; contact dabs sit exactly under their owners.

**Mood:** a floor with a past — walked on, blown over, settled.

**Tell:** four layers, each single-purpose, and the smears all lean the
same way. Directional agreement is what makes painted ground feel like
weather happened here.

# THE DESCRIPTOR BANK — the style's working vocabulary

Use these when building or reviewing any new asset. If an asset can't be
described in these words, it's probably off-style.

**Gesture (one per object):** leaning, sagging, bowing, slumped, swollen,
huddled, jaunty, drowsing, settling, exhaling, listening, bracing, perched,
buried, sprouting, frozen mid-gesture.

**Silhouette:** platter-faceted, countable chords, one long shoulder, broken
stepped side, sagging spine roofline, lumpy bunch-of-grapes, tide-line
ragged, scissor-cut irregular, octagonal honest.

**Surface / material promise:** hand-laid, piped, hammered, sewn, draped,
thrown (blanket), carved-from-one-block, stained by old rain, moss-seasoned,
foot-polished, wind-swept, fondant-soft, skim-milk glassy, velvet-flat.

**Value / hue:** bitter chocolate, cocoa, warm taupe, cream-white, bone,
butter-bright, honey, amber, marigold, skim-milk blue, steel blue, bruise
blue, breath-pale, swamp-green, verdigris, ember orange, rust, crimson
velvet, teal, mauve, violet deep, near-black mottle (never pure black).

**Edge policies:** hard-and-honest (facet seams), hand-wobbled hem (snow,
drift), feathered-to-zero (bloom, pools), crisp-with-rim (cold glow),
chamfered (wall corners), eroded-soft (old stone), ragged tide-line
(organic mass), scissor-irregular (flat graphics).

**Wear / time:** blotch that disobeys the grid, recess-shade dabs, speckle
age-spots, moss on sky-facing planes only, wear-path where feet go, stain
streaks under rims, fractured base keyed into ground.

**Placement grammar:** buried base (no contact lines), plinth under
everything, coping cap palest at every edge, pools chained as paths,
emblem centered like a crest, clusters not grids, thickest-at-corners
(icicles), always an under-seam under an overhang.

**Air / staging:** slow drifters by the dozen, bloom that eats the fixture,
far things soft and pale, near things sharp, big far cut by small near,
dark mass at the frame edge, sky as a flat gradient, horizon as stacked
occlusion cards.

**Tone words:** hand-built, time-worn, pool-lit, storybook, signed like a
poster, cozy with one wrongness, cathedral of sugar, midnight inhabited.
