# w4 — FORGOTTEN CITY (ash-grey monochrome ruins, dark mouths) — HOW evidence

Source: 12 stills (`frames/w4-forgotten-*.jpg`) + full video `/tmp/rt_blinx2/w4-forgotten-full.mp4`
(586 s, verified present) → frames pulled at t=15…575 (`crops/w4-t*.jpg`), 22 pixel crops
(`crops/w4c-*.jpg`). Time-power frames (REW magenta wash t250, cutscenes t545/t575, still-12)
are used only as X-rays of underlying geometry, not as world colour.

The world in one line of construction: **a two-value ash world (pale sage-grey masses /
umber darks) where every saturated pixel is gameplay** — and where "ruin" is painted
(chipped rims, streak staining, incised friezes), never modelled beyond rounded bulges.

---

## Ground (evidence: crops/w4c-ground.jpg, w4c-stripes.jpg, w4c-gemglow.jpg, w4c-bigmouth.jpg)

- Base layer A: **dried-mud crackle tile** — a dense all-over network of thin dark crack
  lines on a mid-grey base, high frequency, reads as desiccated lake bed (w4c-ground).
- Base layer B (other zones): **fine speckle gravel** — tiny dark pits peppered on
  mid-grey, even higher frequency, used in courtyard areas (w4c-gemglow, frames/w4-forgotten-09.jpg).
- Macro layer: **big soft pale drifts** — off-white blotches with soft irregular edges that
  IGNORE the crack grid (crackle continues inside the patch, only the value lifts)
  (w4c-ground, top-right). Confirms the three-layer paint law: macro layer disrespects
  the base tile = "weathered", not "tiled".
- Directional layer unique to this world: **long swept-ash smears** — soft dark ribbons
  (~15–20% darker than base, very soft edges) dragged in parallel diagonals across open
  floors, like wind-rowed ash or flood drag marks (w4c-stripes). A fourth painted layer
  that gives the flat floor a wind direction.
- Baked shadow: overhangs and masses drop **huge soft dark polygonal blobs** on the
  ground (w4c-bigmouth, centre) — soft-edged, ~30% value vs ~60% ground. Plus small
  contact blobs under character and every bomb (w4c-ground under Blinx,
  w4c-mouthfringe under the purple bomb).
- FUNDAMENTAL: the floor carries FOUR painted layers (crackle tile → pale drift →
  dark smear → shadow blobs) on nearly flat geometry. Flatness is hidden by layer
  count, not by displacement.

## Edges & lips (evidence: crops/w4c-rewsteps.jpg, w4c-steps.jpg, w4c-bridge.jpg)

- Platform orientation stepping survives even the REW magenta wash: **treads pale,
  risers clearly darker** (w4c-rewsteps) — the up-face/side-face value rule confirmed
  for this world. Risers additionally carry a darker rubble-block texture with painted
  block divisions.
- Step nosings have a **thin pale worn rim** — each tread edge catches a lighter line,
  reading as chipped/worn stone (w4c-steps). Walkable treads inherit the dark
  ash/rubble ground texture; risers stay paler stone, so "where you can stand" reads
  by texture, not just value.
- Beams/bridges: **top face a step lighter than front face**, and where the beam meets
  masonry there is a **painted dark under-band** (fake AO) gluing it to the wall
  (w4c-bridge). No lip rolls needed at this camera distance — the value step + under-band
  does the work.
- FUNDAMENTAL: edges here are VALUE STEPS + painted grime bands; the poly budget goes
  to the big bulging masses, not to edge geometry.

## Transitions (evidence: crops/w4c-mouthfringe.jpg, w4c-treeroots.jpg, w4c-bridge.jpg, crops/w4-p355.jpg)

- Giant trunk bases get a **jagged dark root-fringe alpha strip** — thin curved tendril
  silhouettes flaring out at ground contact, dark-against-mid (w4c-treeroots). Same
  trick as Everwinter's icicles, inverted in value and function (roots, not drips).
- Dark ground-mouths are rimmed with a **white jagged fringe** — tooth-like pale shapes
  lining the lip where pale wall meets dark opening, light-against-dark so the hole
  reads at any distance (w4c-mouthfringe; also along wall bases in frames/w4-forgotten-01.jpg).
- Foliage alpha tufts are tucked exactly at structural joints: beam ends, wall bases,
  ledge seams (w4c-bridge, w4c-steps top-right) — softening every hard meeting with a
  saturated-green tuft.
- Pits in the floor are crisp-edged dark holes, but always given a **prop** (ladder
  descending — w4-p355) so the hole reads as a place, not a texture bug.
- FUNDAMENTAL: no naked intersections, and the transition element is VALUE-STAGED —
  pale fringe on dark holes, dark fringe on pale ground, green tufts on grey joints.

## Walls & masses (evidence: crops/w4c-crackwall.jpg, w4c-wallslots.jpg, w4c-relief.jpg, w4c-air.jpg, w4c-steps.jpg)

- **Texture scale is the zoning dial**: ground = fine dense crackle; big walls = HUGE
  soft mottled blobs (40–80 px at 640×360) + only sparse long crack strokes
  (w4c-crackwall vs w4c-ground). Vertical surfaces are deliberately lower-frequency
  than floors — mass reads as mass.
- Painted staining: **vertical dark water-streaks** run down pale cliff faces
  (w4c-steps background, w4c-bigmouth right side) — thin dark drips that sell height
  and age with zero geometry.
- The "colonnade" architecture is a **flat wall + painted slot grooves**: tall
  rounded-top vertical slots grouped 3–4, each groove = dark stroke + pale inner edge,
  divided into tiers by continuous horizontal band lines with a darker under-edge
  (w4c-wallslots, w4c-bridge). Repetition is perfectly regular → texture, not geometry.
- Spiral friezes on standing stones are **incised paint**: one dark groove line paired
  with an offset pale highlight line on its upper-left = carved read under one light
  direction; the slab around it only carries speckle + pits (w4c-relief, w4c-air lower-left).
- Masses are **rounded, bulging towers** that lean and taper (w4c-air, frames/w4-forgotten-08.jpg) —
  Seuss elasticity, nothing axis-aligned. Silhouette does the architecture; paint does the age.

## Special surfaces — toxic water (evidence: crops/w4c-secret2.jpg, w4c-canal.jpg, w4c-bridge.jpg, frames/w4-forgotten-10.jpg)

- The canal/pool liquid is **OPAQUE flat saturated lime-green** — nothing continues
  below the surface, no transparency visible (w4c-canal, w4c-bridge).
  **CONTRADICTION of the cross-world water law** (0.6 alpha + submerged walls): that
  law is for clean water; this world's slime is an opaque, emissive-leaning plane.
- Surface detail = **scattered white horizontal dash streaks** lying on the green
  (w4c-secret2) — the same "animated bright streaks" trick, short dashes instead of
  long glints, reading as oily scum drift.
- Shoreline behaviour: the green simply STOPS crisp against dark canal walls; the
  walls above the line are dark umber, so the saturated plane pops by adjacency
  (w4c-bridge). No foam, no meniscus band resolved at 640×360 (HYPOTHESIS: a thin
  darker rim may exist below capture resolution).
- The green pool doubles as a **threshold lure**: the secret room is first seen THROUGH
  a window opening, framed as a pool of saturated green inside grey (crops/w4-t430.jpg).

## Darkness (evidence: crops/w4c-windowrim.jpg, w4c-fog.jpg, w4c-windows.jpg, w4c-mouthfringe.jpg, w4c-bones.jpg, crops/w4-p355.jpg)

- Four distinct darkness devices, chosen by aperture size:
  1. **Window holes** — flat near-#000 arch-topped or rounded-rect punctures; no
     interior gradient resolved. What saves them from "rendering bug" is the
     **pale chipped-plaster rim** — an off-white broken edge ringing the opening,
     strongest on the lit side, proving wall thickness (w4c-windowrim). Around some
     arches a darker grey painted reveal band insets the hole further (w4c-fog).
     NUANCE to the "never pure black" law: pure black IS used, but only as small
     punctures, and the rim — not a gradient — carries the proof of depth.
  2. **Clustered tower windows** — holes placed 2–4 per tower face at varied heights,
     irregular sizes (w4c-air, w4c-windows); one window doubles as a lit display case
     with a pale item visible inside the black (w4c-windows, centre) — darkness as a
     showcase foil.
  3. **Ground pits** — flat near-black, crisp edge, always with a ladder prop
     (w4-p355).
  4. **Dark zones** — NOT black: whole rooms drop to ~15–20% value in WARM umber/olive,
     with soft pale patches on the floor and pale glowing bone-shard props
     (w4c-bones, w4c-treeroots, frames/w4-forgotten-07.jpg). Darkness here is a
     warmer, smoother texture set (cracks vanish, mottle stays) plus value crush.
- Wall-base mouths combine device 4's value crush with the white tooth-fringe rim
  (w4c-mouthfringe).

## Lights (evidence: crops/w4c-gemglow.jpg, w4c-crackwall.jpg, w4c-bones.jpg, crops/w4-t127.jpg, w4c-bigmouth.jpg, w4c-air.jpg)

- No dynamic lights observed anywhere. The full fixture stack is reserved for pickups:
  **saturated mesh + white-core sprite + coloured halo sprite ~2–3× the item** —
  yellow stars with white-hot cores, orange crystal with blown core, cyan crescent,
  green gem with painted top highlight (w4c-gemglow).
- Gold collectibles float unsupported with a small gold halo (w4c-crackwall top) —
  the halo, not a light, does the "glowing".
- Dark zones get **pale bone/crystal shards with soft yellow aura pools** painted/
  sprited on the floor around them (w4c-bones) — the world's only "lamp" is a glowing
  relic on the ground.
- Pink/red glow pools sit near machines (w4-t127) — painted ground pools again.
- The shop is a physical THING: a **white van with yellow crates** (w4c-bigmouth top),
  the warmest structural cluster in the world — safety/goal by temperature.
- The "celestial" is a physical light too: a pale-yellow **hot-air balloon with a
  painted face** (dot eyes, mouth, converging rigging lines) floating over the ruins,
  emissive-look via pale value + painted shading (w4c-air, w4c-sky).

## Sky & distance (evidence: crops/w4c-sky.jpg, w4c-fog.jpg, w4c-air.jpg, frames/w4-forgotten-08.jpg)

- Sky = **flat warm orange-amber painted band** with slight horizontal banding
  (w4c-sky). The drama is inversion: hanging from the top of frame is a fringe of
  **near-black jagged silhouettes** (dead pines / stalactite canopy), the darkest
  values in the whole world, spiking against the orange (w4c-sky).
- CONTRADICTION/nuance to "distance = discrete steps lighter": outdoor depth here is
  built **dark-on-bright** — pale mid-ground towers with black windows, then the black
  canopy fringe, then orange sky. No fog tint stepping between towers is visible;
  occlusion + value inversion does depth (w4c-sky, w4c-air). (The black fringe may be
  a near-camera overhang rather than a far ridge — compositionally it is the dark
  frame device either way.)
- The celestial body is the face-balloon (above) instead of a painted moon.

## Overhead (evidence: crops/w4-t127.jpg, w4c-ladder.jpg, w4c-sky.jpg, w4c-stalactite.jpg, crops/w4-p355.jpg)

- **Wire runs**: 3–4 parallel pale cables strung straight across gorges, spanning the
  full canyon width; enemies climb/hang on them (w4-t127, w4c-ladder — the full-width
  horizontal lines are cables, confirmed by their absence once the camera drops in
  w4-t133). The "built places need wires" law holds even in a dead city.
- Outdoor tops: the black jagged canopy fringe (w4c-sky). Interior tops: **white
  stalactite forms plus black hanging spikes** at the frame top of the secret room
  (w4c-stalactite).
- Below-grade verticality: floor pits with ladders (w4-p355), the green canal sunk
  under bridges (t370, w4c-bridge) — the top third and the bottom cut both carry space.

## Props & motifs (evidence: crops/w4c-steps.jpg, w4c-railing.jpg, w4c-gemglow.jpg, w4c-ladder.jpg, w4c-bigmouth.jpg, crops/w4-t133.jpg)

- Bombs: dark purple bodies with a **painted white specular dome cap** — the highlight
  is IN the diffuse texture, no real specular (w4c-steps, w4c-bridge). Scattered in
  clusters along paths and ledges = breadcrumb rhythm you can also shoot.
- Railings: rust-orange bars with **painted vertical grain streaks and darker bar
  bottoms** (w4c-railing) — wood/rust read from flat boxes; the warmest man-made
  material allowed.
- Green monsters: saturated lime bodies with painted darker-green spots (w4c-ladder) —
  enemies obey the "only gameplay is saturated" ration.
- Jewel pickups (stars, gems, crescents) in magenta/gold/cyan/green (w4c-gemglow,
  frames/w4-forgotten-09.jpg) — candy scattered on ash.
- Spiral-frieze standing stones repeat as a motif (w4c-relief, w4c-air).
- Red cloth banner accent (w4-t133, bottom-right) — rare warm fabric.
- Prop clustering: bombs 2–4 per ledge, windows 2–4 per tower, slots 3–4 per group —
  the "three is a place" rhythm everywhere.

## Particles & air (evidence: crops/w4-t15.jpg, frames/w4-forgotten-01.jpg, crops/w4-p355.jpg vs w4-p357.jpg)

- Pickup/event sparkles: small bright quads near glows (pink motes in w4-t15 and
  frames/w4-forgotten-01.jpg).
- **No camera-wrapped weather layer is resolvable** — frame pairs 1 s apart
  (w4-p355/w4-p357) show no moving speck field at 640×360. HYPOTHESIS: faint drifting
  ash motes may exist below capture resolution; the world's "air" is otherwise carried
  by the umber value-atmosphere of dark zones and the orange sky band, not by a
  particle volume. If implementing, a very sparse pale-grey mote field would fit the
  fiction but is NOT evidence-backed.

## Color script (evidence: all above; frames/w4-forgotten-04/05/08/09)

- Ash-grey/sage family ~60–70% of frame; warm umber darks ~20%; toxic/foliage green
  ~10%; rust orange ~5%; jewel pickup tones ~5%.
- **ALLOWED saturated**: interactive things (pickups, enemies, balloon) + the toxic
  canal + foliage tufts + rust railings. Everything structural stays grey.
- **Shadow hue is WARM** — darks go umber/olive, never blue or violet
  (w4c-mouthfringe, w4c-treeroots, w4c-steps dark masses). Confirms "shadow = world
  atmosphere hue" with a new data point (dead city = warm dust shadow).
- Zone-to-zone contrast: pale ash courts → umber dark zones → saturated green canal
  cuts; t370 stacks all three in one frame (grey walkway, dark canal walls, green water).

## Staging (evidence: crops/w4c-treeroots.jpg, w4c-sky.jpg, crops/w4-t430.jpg, crops/w4-t370.jpg, w4-p355.jpg)

- Dark-frame → bright-core is composed with the world's own props: near-black trunk
  mass framing the right edge against a mid-value floor (w4c-treeroots); black canopy
  spikes crowning the orange sky (w4c-sky).
- Thresholds as hue lures: the secret room's saturated GREEN is first shown through a
  grey window frame (w4-t430) — destination colour through the doorway, exactly the
  cross-world law.
- Verticality: canal below grade + bridge + slotted towers + balloon overhead
  (t370 → w4c-air); floor pits with ladders drop space downward too (w4-p355).
- Path readability: walkable treads carry the dark ash texture; bombs and gems form
  dotted lines along the route (t370, frames/w4-forgotten-09.jpg).

## Feel

**Ash-choked Pompeii toy-box** — a dead city holding its breath; monochrome grief that
lets every candy-coloured thing you can touch feel obscene and precious at once.

## Engine translation (Three.js rules for THIS world)

1. **Four-layer ground shader/canvas**: tileable crackle canvas → overlay 3–5 huge soft
   pale drift blobs (screen-space decals or a second multiply-free overlay) → paint
   2–3 long directional dark smear ribbons per open area → per-object soft blob shadow
   decals. Flat geometry; layer count does the work.
2. **Orientation value bake**: per-vertex or per-face bake — up-faces ~+25% value,
   verticals mid, plus painted dark under-bands baked into wall textures where beams
   attach. Warm umber (#4a4136-ish) as the darken target, never grey/black.
3. **Two-fringe alpha-card kit**: (a) white tooth-fringe strip texture to rim dark
   ground-mouths and wall bases; (b) dark root-fringe strip for trunk/mass bases.
   Both are single repeating alpha textures on quads, staged light-against-dark /
   dark-against-light respectively.
4. **Black punctures with chipped rims**: windows/pits = pure-black inset planes, each
   ringed by a painted pale broken-plaster rim in the wall texture (or a rim decal).
   Cluster 2–4 per mass at varied heights; use one as a lit display case with an
   emissive item inside.
5. **Toxic water**: opaque plane, flat saturated lime with MeshBasicMaterial (no
   lights), plus ~20 short white dash sprites scrolling slowly on the surface. No
   transparency, no reflection. Crisp shoreline against dark walls.
6. **Saturation gate**: keep every structural material under ~15% saturation; only
   pickups/enemies/water/foliage/rust-railings may exceed it. Pickups = saturated mesh
   + additive white-core sprite + additive coloured halo 2–3× mesh size.
7. **Sky + dark crown**: skybox/canvas = flat orange-amber band; hang a near-black
   jagged canopy alpha strip across the top of outdoor arenas (inverted silhouette).
   Add a pale-yellow balloon prop with a painted face as the celestial body.
8. **Painted age**: wall canvas textures get vertical dark drip streaks + huge soft
   mottle at LOW frequency (walls lower-frequency than floors); incised frieze =
   dark stroke + 1–2 px offset pale stroke on flat slabs; colonnades = grouped painted
   slot grooves with horizontal tier bands — no real relief geometry anywhere.

### Cross-world notes (confirmations / contradictions for `_fundamentals.md`)

- CONFIRMS three-layer paint, macro-layer-ignores-grid; orientation value stepping;
  painted contact darkening; fixture stack on pickups; thresholds as hue lures;
  wires in built places; repetition rhythm; warm = goal (white/yellow shop van).
- NUANCE: "never pure black" — pure-black punctures ARE used at small aperture when a
  pale chipped rim proves wall thickness. Rim substitutes for gradient.
- CONTRADICTS the water law: toxic liquid = opaque emissive-leaning plane + dash
  streaks; transparency reserved for clean water only.
- CONTRADICTS "distance = steps lighter": outdoor depth here is dark-on-bright
  (black canopy vs orange sky, pale towers) with no visible fog stepping.
- NEW DATA POINT: shadow hue = warm umber (dead-city dust), extending the
  "shadow = atmosphere hue" law beyond green/violet/blue examples.
