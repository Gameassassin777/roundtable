# w8-forge — THE FORGE (lava industrial, fire, color-zoned rooms)

Evidence base: 12 stills (frames/w8-forge-01…12.jpg) + 14 full frames pulled from
/tmp/rt_blinx2/w8-forge-full.mp4 (crops/w8f-t2…t127.jpg; video present, 130s,
one full Stage-1 run ending in a results screen) + 18 close-up crops
(crops/w8f-*.jpg). The clip is a single stage, so the zone sequence below is
real and ordered: dark rust room → lava overlook → sulphur-yellow beam room →
green corridor → acid-green room → bronze chain room → dark arena →
lime machine room → lava-river hero room → dark retry room.

Fundamentals confirmed/contradicted here are flagged CF/CT. Everything cited is
pixel-verified at 3x unless marked HYPOTHESIS.

## Ground (evidence: crops/w8f-wires.jpg, crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg, crops/w8f-lava1.jpg, crops/w8f-greenroom.jpg)

- WHAT: three distinct floor types — metal walkway, stone arena, cobble shore.
- HOW (walkway): fine DIAMOND-PLATE texture — a regular grid of short diagonal
  dashes, dark brown on near-black (crops/w8f-wires.jpg). Over the grid sit big
  soft dark blotches that ignore the dash pattern (macro layer), and under the
  fire a bright yellow-orange painted POOL with a pale grey-green tapering
  streak beside it (the fixture's ground pool — painted, not lit). CF:
  three-layer paint job confirmed on metal, not just brick/snow.
- HOW (arena): a circular platform carries CONCENTRIC PAINTED ARCS —
  alternating pale-olive / dark-olive ring wedges with a lighter rim band at
  the platform edge (crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg). The pattern is flat texture; the
  circle reads because the painted arcs share one center. The character stands
  on the palest wedge — path legibility by value patch.
- HOW (cobble shore): at the lava pool's near bank the floor becomes rounded
  cobbles, EACH STONE carrying a hot orange-yellow dab on its top face
  (crops/w8f-lava1.jpg) — lava light baked per-stone into the ground texture.
  This is the shore transition AND the spill bake in one layer.
- FUNDAMENTAL: floor richness = fine tiling base + macro blotches + painted
  light pools. The hot dabs on the cobbles are the giveaway: light is painted
  PER-ELEMENT, not cast.

## Edges & lips (evidence: crops/w8f-beams.jpg, crops/w8f-hatch.jpg, crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg)

- WHAT: structural beams read as bolted steel; floor hatches read as heavy
  cast rings; platform rims read rounded.
- HOW (beams): the orange I-beams are FLAT single-color plates with mitered
  45° corners (crops/w8f-beams.jpg). All relief is painted INTO the texture:
  each rivet = a red-orange circle + dark-red lower crescent + lighter hot
  dab top-left (one consistent light direction), plus a thin dark seam line
  along the beam's inner edge. Zero rivet geometry.
- HOW (hatch ring): the round floor hatch is a red torus-like ROLL with a
  painted lighter-red highlight arc top-left and darker bottom (crops/w8f-hatch.jpg);
  its interior is a recessed dark grate of real spoke geometry — the only
  place spokes are modeled, because they silhouette against the pit.
- HOW (arena rim): platform edge gets a lighter value band before the drop
  (crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg) — rim-light by paint, and the drop beyond falls to
  murk, not black (see Darkness).
- FUNDAMENTAL: machined metal = flat color + painted rivets (dark crescent
  down, hot dab up-left). Curved cast metal = ring roll + painted highlight
  arc. Geometry is only spent where it breaks silhouette.

## Transitions (evidence: crops/w8f-hazard.jpg, crops/w8f-lava1.jpg, crops/w8f-wires.jpg)

- WHAT: nothing touches anything naked; three transition species appear.
- HOW (fog-gap): where the intro structure parts, the gap is filled with a
  saturated YELLOW-GREEN GRADIENT — brightest yellow near the grey beam,
  greening left, dissolving into the dark brown ground (crops/w8f-hazard.jpg).
  A concrete block painted with BROKEN red/white hazard chevrons (irregular
  red slabs with dark outlines, not crisp tape) is wedged in the glow — the
  glow both hides the mesh seam and motivates the warning paint.
- HOW (shore): floor→lava is mediated by the hot-dabbed cobble strip
  (crops/w8f-lava1.jpg) — a material band, never a hard texture swap.
- HOW (contact): props sit on painted soft dark blobs / glow pools
  (crops/w8f-wires.jpg under the fire; frames/w8-forge-01.jpg under the yellow
  markers). CF: painted contact darkening confirmed.
- FUNDAMENTAL: in the Forge the seam-hider is often LIGHT (green fog, hot
  cobbles) rather than dirt — transitions are done with glow, not just grunge.

## Walls & masses (evidence: crops/w8f-yellowwall.jpg, crops/w8f-tornado.jpg, crops/w8f-chain.jpg, crops/w8f-rustwall.jpg, crops/w8f-limemachine.jpg)

- WHAT: four wall treatments, one per zone, at three different texture scales.
- HOW (sulphur room): ochre wall = HUGE soft blotches + vertical drip streaks
  running down from the top band (crops/w8f-yellowwall.jpg); horizontal timber
  beams cross it, painted dark brown with lighter grain dashes and a metal
  strap. No fine grid anywhere — the scale is primal/organic.
- HOW (bronze room): large brown panels + darker brown blob shading + small
  dark rectangles (painted rivets/panel marks); a thin horizontal PIPE of real
  geometry runs across the wall with a pale highlight along its top edge and
  tiny brackets (crops/w8f-chain.jpg, crops/w8f-tornado.jpg); a ladder is pure
  wall texture — dark verticals with lighter rung dabs (crops/w8f-chain.jpg
  right edge). CF: texture bands + thin real ledges, here with the pipe as
  the silhouette element.
- HOW (rust room): dark red-brown panels pushed so dark the seams barely read;
  one small pale window rectangle with a dark grate (crops/w8f-rustwall.jpg).
  Legibility is sacrificed deliberately — this is the "rest" room before the
  lava reveal.
- HOW (machine masses): the lime-room machinery is painted with the LARGEST
  blobs in the world — soft dark-olive shapes several meters across, zero
  fine detail (crops/w8f-limemachine.jpg). CF: texture scale as mood dial —
  machine = huge blobs (primal mass), panels = medium, diamond plate = fine
  (ordered walkway). The player reads "walkable vs mass" from texture scale
  alone.
- FUNDAMENTAL: each room gets ONE wall recipe (scale + stain direction);
  variety comes from zoning, not from mixing recipes within a room.

## Special surfaces — LAVA (evidence: crops/w8f-lava1.jpg, crops/w8f-lavariver.jpg, crops/w8f-lavabank.jpg, crops/w8f-pitlava.jpg)

- WHAT: two lava moods — a yellow-white pool (t20) and a deep-red river with
  yellow veins (t100) — plus crust islands floating on both.
- HOW (surface): crackle-cell texture, white-yellow cores → orange → deep
  maroon-red borders (crops/w8f-lavariver.jpg). Fully OPAQUE and emissive —
  CT: contradicts the water recipe (alpha ~0.6, wall visible below); lava is
  a light SOURCE, not a lens, so it gets no transparency at all.
- HOW (crust islands): dark maroon "plates" float on the surface, and EACH
  PLATE IS OUTLINED WITH A THIN PALE RIM (light tan line, crops/w8f-lavabank.jpg
  top-left). A dark blob alone would read as a hole; the pale rim is what
  sells "cooled solid skin riding on liquid". No geometry needed.
- HOW (meniscus): the brightest yellow crackle is forced to the banks — at
  the ground boundary the cells run white-hot (crops/w8f-lavabank.jpg
  top-left corner; far bank in crops/w8f-lava1.jpg). CF: hot meniscus
  confirmed; here it's clearly a painted edge band of hotter cells, not a
  separate mesh.
- HOW (spill): pit walls near lava carry a vertical red-orange gradient wash
  (crops/w8f-pitlava.jpg); shore cobbles carry per-stone hot dabs
  (crops/w8f-lava1.jpg). Two bake radii: broad wash on walls, per-element
  dabs on the ground.
- HOW (two moods): HYPOTHESIS — pool vs river differ by texture variant and
  emissive level (hot start vs cooled downstream), not by shader; both share
  the same cell logic.
- HOW (overhangs): dark green-black vertical drips/stalactite silhouettes hang
  above the lava, backlit by it (crops/w8f-lavabank.jpg top edge) — dark
  masses staged AGAINST the brightest surface for silhouette.
- FUNDAMENTAL: lava = opaque emissive crackle + pale-rimmed crust cards +
  forced-hot bank band + two-radius baked spill. The crust rims are the
  world-specific trick worth stealing.

## Darkness (evidence: crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg, crops/w8f-hazard.jpg, crops/w8f-doorway.jpg, frames/w8-forge-11.jpg)

- WHAT: pits and voids in the Forge are frequently NOT dark — they glow.
- HOW: the structural void under the intro rig is a saturated green gradient
  (crops/w8f-hazard.jpg); the arena's surrounding pit is olive-brown murk at
  ~20% value, never #000 (crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg, CF); the retry room is near-
  black brown with the red hatch ring as its only saturated object
  (frames/w8-forge-11.jpg). Doorway darkness is skipped entirely in favor of
  the next room's blaze (crops/w8f-doorway.jpg).
- FUNDAMENTAL: in a fire world the underworld GLOWS — "dark" voids are
  replaced by colored fog glow (green) so the palette never leaves the
  warm/acid family. When true darkness does appear it's ~20% olive or maroon,
  i.e. shadow-as-hue (CF) with the hue being olive/maroon here.

## Lights (evidence: crops/w8f-grate.jpg, crops/w8f-embers.jpg, crops/w8f-wires.jpg, crops/w8f-greenroom.jpg, frames/w8-forge-12.jpg)

- WHAT: furnace grates, colored vents, open flame, sweep beams — every light
  is a physical THING (CF).
- HOW (furnace grate): arched window in a dark mass; a diamond-crosshatch
  lattice painted over a yellow-white emissive gradient, hottest at center,
  cooling to gold at the arch edges (crops/w8f-grate.jpg). Lattice + emissive
  gradient = "something burns behind this" with zero geometry behind it.
- HOW (vents): saturated LIME-GREEN rectangles set flush in bronze walls
  (crops/w8f-embers.jpg top-right) — flat emissive quads; the acid zones leak
  into the bronze zone through them.
- HOW (open flame): white-yellow additive core with orange fringe, plus a
  painted yellow-orange floor pool and a pale tapering streak
  (crops/w8f-wires.jpg; frames/w8-forge-01.jpg). CF: the fixture stack minus
  the halo sprite (halos appear on pickups instead).
- HOW (sweep beam): the time-sweep is a vertical additive column, white core
  fringed green by the room (crops/w8f-greenroom.jpg).
- HOW (breadcrumb): small warm lamp posts and glow pickups receding through
  the dark room (frames/w8-forge-12.jpg) — CF breadcrumb trail.
- FUNDAMENTAL: no fixture lights anything dynamically; glow lives in the
  emissive texture + painted pool. Grate + vent are this world's signature
  fixtures: glow behind industry.

## Sky & distance (evidence: crops/w8f-doorway.jpg, crops/w8f-rustwall.jpg)

- No sky anywhere — the Forge is fully interior; the video never shows a
  ceiling opening. Distance is handled by: (a) per-room fog tint (green in
  acid rooms, brown-black in rust rooms), and (b) the DOORWAY AS HORIZON —
  the next room's saturated hue framed in a plain rectangular cut is the only
  "vista" (crops/w8f-doorway.jpg). HYPOTHESIS: longer sightlines exist later
  in the world (the stage here is corridor-scale); the clip can't resolve a
  true big-space treatment.

## Overhead (evidence: crops/w8f-beams.jpg, crops/w8f-chain.jpg, crops/w8f-wires.jpg, frames/w8-forge-06.jpg)

- WHAT: the top third is occupied by beams, pipes, chains, wires — industry
  overhead instead of sky (CF: upper third never empty).
- HOW: orange mitered I-beams with painted rivets frame the top of corridors
  (crops/w8f-beams.jpg); thin pipes run wall-to-wall with a painted top
  highlight and brackets (crops/w8f-chain.jpg); chains hang from off-screen
  with a white highlight dab per link (frames/w8-forge-06.jpg); in the retry

- FUNDAMENTAL: overhead clutter = long thin silhouette elements (wire, pipe,

  small; they're near-straight lines that simply prove a ceiling exists.

- WHAT: sparse embers, sweep beams, and the global time-control VFX layer.

- HOW (fire): character fire = white core + orange fringe additive stack plus
  painted floor pool (crops/w8f-wires.jpg).
- HOW (time VFX): rewind/fast-forward renders as full-screen HORIZONTAL SHEAR
  LINES — 2–3px bright scanlines with slight row offsets (crops/w8f-tornado.jpg,
  crops/w8f-t90 region); the time-tornado is a twisted ribbon mesh, white on
  one face and PINK on the other (two-tone vertex color), additive
  (crops/w8f-tornado.jpg).
- FUNDAMENTAL: ambient air = fog tint + a handful of motes; spectacle is
  reserved for the time powers, and it reads as a VIDEO-GLITCH (scanline
  shear) — the world's "weather" is broadcast interference.

## Color script (evidence: frames/w8-forge-01…12.jpg, crops/w8f-doorway.jpg, crops/w8f-limemachine.jpg)

- Hue families, rough share of the clip: rust red-brown ~30%, olive/acid green
  ~25%, lava orange-gold ~20%, dark bronze brown ~15%, saturated accents
  (violet bombs, pink checks, cyan HUD) ~10%. CF: 5 families max.
- SHADOW HUE: shadows are OLIVE-BROWN in green/bronze zones and MAROON in the
  rust zone — never grey (crops/w8f-darkpit.jpg olive murk; crops/w8f-rustwall.jpg
  maroon panels). CF: shadow-as-hue; the Forge's shadow is "cold lava".
- Allowed saturated: lava, green fog-glow, furnace grates/vents, pickups,
  enemies, UI. Everything built stays mid-value.
- ZONE-TO-ZONE: the clip is a hue ladder — maroon rust → sulphur yellow →
  acid green → bronze → lime+gold → lava orange. The green-corridor →
  orange-room doorway (crops/w8f-doorway.jpg, frames/w8-forge-04.jpg) is the
  exact instance _fundamentals.md cites — CONFIRMED on disk: a plain
  rectangular cut, no trim, with the destination's saturated orange doing 100%
  of the lure work, and small sparkle motes on the threshold as punctuation.
- NEW vs fundamentals: this world color-codes ROOMS wholesale (one dominant
  hue per room) rather than accenting within a shared palette — the palette
  itself resets at every threshold.

## Feel

Sulphur and sweat — a claustrophobic boiler-room maze where the heat is the
only light and every doorway promises a different-colored oven.

## Engine translation (Three.js rules for THIS world)

1. **Lava material**: canvas-generated Voronoi crackle texture (white core →
   orange → maroon border), `MeshBasicMaterial` with emissive-looking full-bright
   color, slow UV scroll; paint a forced-hot cell band along the bank edges
   (meniscus strip). Scatter 5–10 dark "crust plate" quads per pool, each with
   a painted pale rim in its texture, drifting slowly. Opaque — never
   transparent (lava is a source, not a lens).
2. **Two-radius spill bake**: vertex-color pass — broad red-orange wash on all
   wall vertices within ~4m of lava (falloff by distance), PLUS per-element hot
   dabs painted into shore/floor textures adjacent to lava (cobble tops). Do
   both; either alone reads half-finished.
3. **Per-room color zones**: each room gets a dominant hue applied three ways
   at once — fog color, ambient/hemisphere tint, and wall texture palette
   (rust #3a1f18 maroon, acid #4a7a2a green, sulphur #8a7a3a, bronze #4a3520).
   Doorways are plain unlit rectangular cuts; the destination room's fog+glow
   showing through IS the lure. Zone change = swap all three at the threshold.
4. **Painted-rivet steel**: beams as flat-colored box/extrusion geometry with
   mitered corners; rivets, seam lines, chevrons, drips ALL in the canvas
   texture (rivet = dark lower crescent + hot top-left dab, one global light
   direction). Spend polygons only on things that silhouette: hatch spoke
   grates, pipes crossing walls, hanging chains.
5. **Glow fixtures**: furnace grate = emissive canvas texture (lattice over a
   radial white→gold gradient) + small additive halo sprite + painted floor
   pool decal. Colored vents = flat emissive quads flush in walls leaking the
   neighboring zone's hue. Keep any real PointLights very gentle.
6. **Glow-pit darkness**: replace black pit floors with a stack of 2–3
   additive radial-gradient sprites (saturated green, brightest center) or a
   fog-tinted ~20%-value murk plane. In fire worlds, voids glow; reserve true
   dark (olive/maroon, never #000) for rest rooms.
7. **Air & time VFX**: low-density camera-facing ember motes near lava and
   thresholds only; room-wide fog tint carries the air. Time powers =
   post-style overlay of horizontal shear scanlines + a two-tone (white/pink)
   additive ribbon mesh for the tornado — broadcast glitch, not weather.
8. **Overhead layer**: cap every corridor with 2–3 thin long elements
   (wire with slight sag, pipe with painted top highlight, hanging chain with
   per-link highlight dabs) so the top third is never empty; ceiling geometry
   itself can stay off-screen.
